# 03 — User Personas

## Primary Persona: David — The Busy Barber

- **Age:** 26
- **Location:** Yerevan
- **Role:** Barber renting a chair in a shared barbershop (2–5 barbers)
- **Monthly income:** ~$600, depends on clientele
- **How he gets clients today:** Instagram, word of mouth, walk-ins
- **How clients book today:** Phone calls, WhatsApp, Telegram, Instagram DM

**Pain points:**
- Constant interruptions — clients call mid-haircut
- Double bookings from juggling multiple channels
- Messy scheduling — relies on notes, memory, or scattered messages
- Clients forget appointments and don't show up
- Time lost in back-and-forth just to agree on a time

**What David needs from On-Time:**
- Something he can start using in minutes, without a tutorial
- A booking link he can drop into his Instagram bio today
- Confidence that two clients can't get the same slot
- To not feel like he's paying for software before he's making money from it

## Secondary Persona: Anahit — The Home-Based Nail Specialist

- Works from home, no shop address, no existing booking system
- Currently only reachable by phone call
- Wants to look more professional and legitimate to attract new clients
  beyond her existing circle
- Portfolio/photos matter more to her than to David — her work is highly
  visual and it's how she wins new clients
- More price-sensitive than David initially, but has strong upside once
  her client base grows

**What Anahit needs from On-Time:**
- A polished public profile that makes her look established even though
  she's small
- Photo/portfolio upload that's simple from a phone
- A reason to trust that a booking platform (not just Instagram) is worth
  her time

## Guest / Client Persona (booking side)

- Books via a link shared on Instagram, WhatsApp, or found through search
- Does not want to create an account just to book a haircut
- Wants to see availability at a glance and confirm in under a minute
- Primarily on mobile
- Comfortable with Armenian or Russian; some comfortable with English

**Design implication:** the guest booking flow is the entire brand
experience for this persona — there is no app, no account, no ongoing
relationship surface beyond this one flow and the reminder message. It has
to build trust fast (looks legitimate, loads fast, feels simpler than an
Instagram DM).

## Adoption Obstacles (apply to both specialist personas)

- Convincing them to create a profile at all — behavior change, not tech
  literacy, is the barrier
- Convincing them to move bookings away from Instagram/WhatsApp habits
- Convincing them to invest time uploading photos and setting up services
  before they've seen any payoff
- Trust that the platform won't lose a booking or double-book them —
  reliability has to be proven immediately, not just claimed
