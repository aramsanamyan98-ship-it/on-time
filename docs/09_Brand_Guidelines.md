# 09 — Brand Guidelines

## Positioning Statement

> For barbers and beauty specialists starting to build their name in
> Armenia, who have no way to be found beyond word of mouth and can't
> afford software before they have steady income, On-Time is a booking
> platform that helps them get discovered and get paid without giving up
> hours to phone calls and DMs. Unlike SetTime and Altegio, which serve
> specialists who already have a client base, On-Time grows with you from
> your first client to your fiftieth — free while you're building,
> affordable once you've made it.

## What Differentiates Us (and what doesn't)

Do **not** lead marketing or product messaging with "reminders" or basic
scheduling — that's table stakes locally (SetTime already does this well,
including via WhatsApp/Telegram). Our real differentiation is:

1. **Discovery** — we help specialists who have no way to be found get
   found. Existing local tools assume you already have clients.
2. **"Grow with you" pricing** — we don't charge until a specialist has
   proof the platform is bringing them clients. This is a stronger
   emotional hook than any feature list, especially for the just-starting
   segment we're targeting.

Because our name ("On-Time") sits close to a direct competitor's name
("SetTime"), the brand's visual identity and messaging need to carry more
of the differentiation than the name itself can. Avoid clock/stopwatch/
checkmark visual clichés that reinforce the name-only association with
"scheduling" — lean into craft/portfolio imagery instead.

## Brand Promise

We promise that barbers and beauty professionals will not waste time
talking to clients — and that we won't charge them before they're actually
earning from the platform.

## Mission

To provide barbers with a simple digital tool that organizes appointments,
reduces interruptions, and helps them run their business more efficiently.

## Core Values

- Simplicity
- Reliability
- Respect for the craft
- Efficiency
- Community

## Brand Personality

**Is:** practical, confident, modern, supportive, straightforward,
punctual

**Is not:** overly corporate, too technical, complicated

## Brand Voice

Simple, direct, friendly, professional. Write like you're talking to a
skilled tradesperson who is busy and doesn't want to read marketing
fluff — not like enterprise software copy.

## Tagline Direction

Lead with the discovery/growth story, not the scheduling mechanic. Example
directions (not final — refine with a copywriter before public launch):

- "Where Armenia discovers its next favorite barber."
- "Built for the specialist who's still building their name."
- "We don't get paid until you do."

## Visual Identity

### Color System

| Color | Hex | Meaning | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Deep Green (primary) | `#1F3D2B` | Stability, control, trust | Primary brand color |
| Charcoal Black | `#121212` | Authority, structure | Text, dark UI elements |
| Soft Gold (accent) | `#C8A96A` | Premium, professionalism | Buttons, highlights, icons |
| Warm White | `#F7F5F2` | Calm, cleanliness | Backgrounds |

Feeling: not flashy, not playful — a premium, trustworthy craft
environment. Note: this palette is common among grooming/barbershop brands
generally (including several existing Yerevan barbershops) — the app's UI
should lean slightly more "capable software" than "lifestyle barbershop"
in its execution, since our buyer is a business owner evaluating a tool,
not a customer choosing a haircut experience. Save the more atmospheric,
lifestyle-heavy visual treatment for the Webflow marketing site, where it
can help specialists picture their own brand looking that polished.

### Typography

- **Headlines:** Sans-serif, strong and modern — Inter, Poppins, or
  Montserrat
- **Body:** Inter (recommended for everything, including UI)
- **Style:** medium-to-bold headlines, clear spacing, no decorative fonts

## Language & Localization Notes

The brand voice must translate consistently across Armenian, Russian, and
English — direct and simple in each, not just a literal translation of
English marketing copy. Avoid idioms that don't survive translation
cleanly (e.g., don't lean on English wordplay for the tagline if it won't
land in Armenian or Russian).

## Naming Note (for future reference)

"On-Time" was kept as the name despite a naming-collision risk with the
Armenian competitor "SetTime" (also a time/scheduling-themed name). This
was a deliberate trade-off to move forward with development rather than
delay for a rename. Because of this, extra discipline is required
everywhere else in the brand system (visual identity, tagline, positioning
in every piece of copy) to prevent the two brands from blurring together
in the market. Do not let a future team member "simplify" the tagline back
down to something time/scheduling-generic — that reintroduces the
collision risk this document was written to manage.
